something I've noticed is how "result" oriented people can be. The largest (?) example that comes to mind is with art. this is something i am guilty of, where I used to focus fully on what i made. unless you are getting paid that is a really great way to get depressed about what you are doing and not enjoy it at all. Its remarkable how hung up on that shit i used to get. but a few months ago i picked up some watercolors and made a really cool bookmark. I only noticed it was cool after i had made it, as i was vibing out to music, and just living in the moment.

something that came up in our single serving internet culture, was a pretty simple phrase,

it was often used in reference to ragebaiting the shit out of some poor soul. but what is this "game"? life itself is often referred to as a game. there's a whole ass best-selling board game. must be true.

Now, I am not here to say I am amazing at whatever I do. I'd go as far to say I suck at a lot of things. But that makes me human, and within these gaps in skill and knowledge is the very material of my being.

Gotta reel things in here, I'm trying to be more cohesive. (unlike that last post)

My point is, there's something deeper to this phrase.

When one focuses on purely the results of whatever they do, their mind will often create a perfect version of this result, something that is not attainable without years of practice and experience. I can imagine what a photorealistic drawing of someone would look like, but I sure as shit couldn't recreate that. And if I were to try, I'd only be disappointed. And that is what I used to do. Not necessarily perfectionism, but needlessly high expectations with a very low level of skill. Not a good combo.

This just one side to the coin. What's to say this whole thing of "enjoy the journey, not the destination" isn't applicable to doing stuff you aren't good at? I'll let you in on a secret, I am not good at web development. I also don't really have a reason to even be making this website. but here I am, putting hours in just because I enjoy it. There's like two people who read this (shoutout Andie and Byron) so I don't do this for internet validation.

If I was getting paid to do this, or had years of experience or was even comparing this to any professionally done website, or hell, even some slopsite cranked out with one hand by some redditor, I'd be disappointed. but here I am, creating. For the love of the game.

If you have fun doing something, the result gets tied to the enjoyment you got out of the process, and whenever you see the result It just reminds you of the time you had. For me at least, this immediately boosts how I feel about what I made.
