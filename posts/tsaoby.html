{introduction}

Personally, I love to make things. Art, robots, and anything in between. there is something satisfying about just making things, and more specifically when something works. Now this isn't super relevant to when I take the time to draw or paint, as I see the art as the process, not the result.

{Impostor among you (its you lol)}

What i dont understand is how I always manage to come up with some bullshit about how i don't have the right tool, part of piece of knowledge to start a project, let alone finish one. I've got a notebook, and in there are about 20 fleshed out ideas for projects. I've started 3 of them, and only one has been finished to a point that I'm happy with. all three projects fall into some categories that in my eyes fit all projects.

type one: you get about halfway through, decide that some constraint you designed around in the beginning was done wrong, and move onto the next iteration. A nerf blaster i was making fell into this trap. I've got 5 versions of this damn thing, only the 5th was somewhat functional.

type two: pseudocomplete. these are the projects that get to a point where they are functional as a prototype, and work well. but for whatever reason you call it a day. no shame here, just an understanding that this wasn't your best.

type three: this is the most glamorized, and for good reason. Congrats! after hours of work you finished the damn thing. and your happy with it. this gives your monkey brain a dopamine hit, making you feel rewarded for your efforts.

{what can I even DO}

now this whole thing of not being enough to do what you want isn't unique to me. (disclaimer here, it is NOT a recognized disorder, or even a disorder for that matter. I'm not self diagnosing with anything here, and neither should you) the term was first coined in a 1978 article "The impostor phenomena in high achieving women: dynamics and therapeutic intervention" by Pauline R. Clance and Suzanne A. Imes. Its described as a "an internal experience of intellectual phoniness". This feeling of not knowing enough to do what i want, paired with honestly terrible discipline creates a great breeding ground for complacency. I don't want that, you don't want that.

What can even be done here? Speaking from experience, being in your own head is the worst place to be if you want to achieve anything in life. at the end of the day, you are your own worst enemy. And what sucks is how the first step is the hardest. No one can wake up one morning and decide that they are some superhuman get-stuff-done machine.

Its hard to get to the top of a mountain without boots, but do you really need your phone?

{you SUCK at keeping yourself accountable}

Take a page from any tech corporation and outsource a job. make a close friends Instagram story, and share what your gonna do that day.

{Who's in charge anyways?}

Evolutionarily, we are designed to be lazy turds. It makes sense, conserve energy, die less, make more humans. but this is the big 26. the best way to do something is to take that first step. genuinely roll out of bed onto the floor. sit down at your keyboard. whip out that pencil.

{a white boy in motion stays in motion}

this is the big part. Starting is hard. Stopping is easy. Coasting? also easy. habits take weeks to make, days to break, but hold up forever.

{Unless acted on by an outside force}

Some "things" in life aren't necessary. a great way to hate yourself and feel like shit is to doom scroll for 5 hours and then force yourself to do something. that shit is designed to make you complacent, content, and controllable. that makes you boring. Who the FUCK wants to be a boring person?

{to be honest is to be free}

Be true to yourself. Its easier to do what you want to do, than to take the time to think about how others will perceive you. they are going to judge you anyways. Any person I'd want to be around isn't someone that's going to judge someone for having fun and showing soul.
